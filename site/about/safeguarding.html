Our commitment

The safety and wellbeing of all students is our absolute priority. I am committed to creating and maintaining a safe learning environment for all students, both online and in any future in-person tutoring.

This policy follows guidance aligned with Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) and reflects best practices for private tutoring services.

✓ Certified and Verified

I hold an enhanced DBS check and complete annual safeguarding training to ensure I'm equipped to recognise and respond to any concerns appropriately.

2

DBS and training

I maintain current and appropriate safeguarding credentials:

  • Enhanced DBS check: Current and renewed as required
  • Safeguarding training: Completed annually through recognised providers
  • Online safety training: Specific training for digital tutoring environments
  • Professional development: Regular updates on safeguarding best practices

All certifications are available for review upon request from parents or guardians.

3

Online lesson safety

All online tutoring sessions include specific safety measures:

  • Secure platforms: Using reputable video conferencing with waiting rooms
  • Controlled access: Only invited participants are admitted to sessions
  • Parental awareness: Parents/guardians should be nearby during sessions for younger students
  • No unexpected recording: Sessions are never recorded without explicit written consent
  • Appropriate communication: All communication remains professional and within tutoring scope
  • Clear boundaries: Sessions focus strictly on educational content
4

Recognising concerns

I am trained to recognise potential indicators of:

  • Physical, emotional, or sexual abuse
  • Neglect or inadequate care
  • Bullying (including cyberbullying)
  • Mental health concerns
  • Radicalisation or extremism
  • Online safety risks

If any concerns arise, I will take appropriate action in line with safeguarding procedures, prioritising the student's wellbeing.

5

Responding to concerns

If a safeguarding concern arises, I will:

  • Listen: Take any disclosure seriously and supportively
  • Record: Document concerns accurately and securely
  • Report: Contact appropriate authorities when required
  • Support: Continue to provide appropriate support to the student
  • Review: Assess whether tutoring should continue and under what conditions

I may pause or discontinue services if safeguarding concerns cannot be adequately addressed.

6

Reporting and emergency contacts

! Emergency Situations

If a child is in immediate danger, call 999 immediately.
For non-emergency child protection concerns, contact your local authority's children's services or the NSPCC helpline: 0808 800 5000.

I am committed to working with parents, guardians, and relevant authorities to ensure all students receive the support and protection they need.
